# Zyrcon Code of Conduct

## Our standard

We want Zyrcon to be a place where people can contribute seriously, disagree productively, and collaborate without hostility.

That means:
- treat other people with respect
- criticize ideas clearly, not people personally
- assume good faith until there is strong reason not to
- keep discussion grounded in evidence, code, and outcomes
- do not harass, threaten, demean, or target others

## Unacceptable behavior

This includes:
- personal attacks
- harassment or intimidation
- hate speech
- sexualized language directed at participants
- repeated bad-faith disruption
- publishing private information without permission
- abusive or insulting behavior that makes collaboration harder

## Expected behavior

Please try to:
- be direct without being hostile
- be specific when giving feedback
- help newcomers orient themselves
- admit uncertainty when you are unsure
- keep the project better than you found it

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in:
- issues
- pull requests
- discussions
- project documentation
- launch or community spaces clearly tied to the project

## Enforcement

Maintainers may:
- ask for a correction
- remove content
- lock conversations
- restrict participation
- ban repeat or severe offenders

## Reporting

Report concerns to: `maintainers@zyrconlabs.com`

When reporting, include:
- what happened
- where it happened
- links or screenshots if available
- any immediate concern for safety or repeated abuse

## Final note

We are building for serious operators. The standard here should feel serious too.
