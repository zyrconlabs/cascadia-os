1. Overview

Zyrcon, Inc. ("Zyrcon," "we," "us," or "our") operates Cascadia OS and the DEPOT operator marketplace. This Privacy Policy describes what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights with respect to that information.

Cascadia OS is built on a local-first architecture: your operator data, conversation content, workflow configurations, and business data remain on your device and are never transmitted to Zyrcon servers.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account Information

When you register for a Zyrcon account, we collect your email address, display name, and password hash. If you authenticate via GitHub OAuth, we receive only your public GitHub profile information (username and email).

2.2 Billing Information

Payments are processed by Stripe, Inc. Zyrcon does not store your full credit card number, CVV, or bank account details. We receive from Stripe a payment token, last-four card digits, billing address, and transaction history for your account.

2.3 Crash Reports (Opt-In)

If you enable crash reporting, Cascadia OS may transmit anonymized stack traces and system metadata (OS version, platform architecture, Cascadia OS version) when an unhandled error occurs. No file contents, operator data, or user-generated content is included. You may disable this at any time in Settings.

2.4 Usage Metrics (Opt-In)

If you enable anonymous usage metrics, we collect aggregate data such as which operator categories are installed (not operator names or contents), session duration, and feature interaction counts. This data is not linked to your identity. You may disable this at any time in Settings.

3. Information We Do Not Collect

Zyrcon does not collect, transmit, or store:

  • The contents of any operator conversation or workflow
  • Business data processed by operators (invoices, leads, CRM records, etc.)
  • Files on your local machine
  • Credentials or API keys you configure within operators
  • Output generated by AI models running locally on your device

This is a foundational commitment of the local-first architecture and is enforced at the platform level.

4. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Create and manage your account
  • Process payments and issue receipts
  • Provide customer support
  • Send transactional emails (account verification, billing receipts, security alerts)
  • Improve platform stability via opt-in crash reports
  • Understand aggregate platform usage via opt-in metrics

We do not sell your personal information to third parties. We do not use your data to train AI models.

6. Data Retention

  • Account data is retained for the duration of your account and for 90 days after deletion.
  • Billing records are retained for 7 years as required by applicable tax laws.
  • Crash reports are retained for 12 months, then deleted.
  • Usage metrics are aggregated and anonymized within 30 days of collection; raw records are deleted.

You may request deletion of your account data at any time by contacting legal@zyrcon.ai.

7. Your Rights

GDPR — EEA & UK Users

Right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, or port your personal data, and to object to processing. Contact legal@zyrcon.ai. We respond within 30 days.

CCPA — California Residents

Right to know what we collect, right to delete, right to opt out of sale (we do not sell personal information). Contact legal@zyrcon.ai or use the in-app Privacy Settings panel.

8. Security

We implement industry-standard security measures including TLS encryption in transit, AES-256 encryption at rest for account data, and regular access reviews. No system is perfectly secure; please notify us immediately at legal@zyrcon.ai if you discover a potential vulnerability.

9. Children's Privacy

Cascadia OS is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we learn we have done so, we will delete that information promptly.

10. Changes to This Policy

We will notify you of material changes via email or in-app notice at least 14 days before the change takes effect. Continued use after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
